Here's a timeline on Stephen Hawking's career:

    Hawking was born on 8 January 1942 in Oxford, England

  • 1950 - Hawking and his family moved to St Albans, Hertfordshire

  • 1952 - Hawking attended Radlett School, an independent school in the village
    of Radlett in Hertfordshire, for a year.

  • 1959 - Hawking was awarded a scholarship after taking the examinations.

  • 1960 -Hawking began his university education at University College,
    Oxford at the age of 17

  • 1962 - After receiving a first-class BA (Hons.) degree in natural science and
    completing a trip to Iran with a friend, he began his graduate work at Trinity Hall, Cambridge.

  • 1964 - Hawking started developing a reputation for brilliance and brashness when he
    publicly challenged the work of Fred Hoyle and his student Jayant Narlikar at a lecture.

  • 1970 - Hawking postulated what became known as the second law of black hole dynamics,
    that the event horizon of a black hole can never get smaller.

  • 1973 - Hawking moved into the study of quantum gravity and quantum mechanics.

  • 1975 -Hawking returned to Cambridge to a more academically senior post,
    as reader in gravitational physics.

  • 1985 -Further work by Hawking in the area of arrows of time publication of a paper
    theorising that if the no-boundary proposition were correct, then when the universe stopped
    expanding and eventually collapsed, time would

    • run backwards.
  • 1993 - he co-edited a book on Euclidean quantum gravity with Gary Gibbons and
    published a collected edition of his own articles on black holes and the Big Bang.

  • 2001 -Hawking continued his writings for a popular audience, publishing The
    Universe in a Nutshell.

  • 2015 - Hawking said that not all information is lost when something enters a black hole
    and there might be a possibility to retrieve information from a black hole according to his theory.

  • 2017 - Hawking was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Imperial College London.
